# 03 — Outreach: sequences that book calls

The entire edge: **you attach proof, not promises.** Every email carries the prospect's own violations from your scan. Nobody ignores a document with their logo-page and "3 critical" on it for long.

Rules: plain text, no images or links in email #1 (deliverability), 4-sentence maximum, one CTA. Send Tu–Th, 8–10am their time.

## Email sequence (attach the scan PDF to email 1)

### Email 1 — the report

> **Subject:** {{Brand}}'s checkout — 3 critical accessibility failures
>
> Hi {{FirstName}},
>
> I ran {{brand.com}} through the accessibility standard EU regulators use (WCAG 2.1 AA — the European Accessibility Act benchmark) and found {{X}} violations, including {{worst finding, plain English — e.g. "product images invisible to screen-reader users"}}. Full report attached — it's yours either way.
>
> Since you sell into the EU, the EAA has applied to {{Brand}} since June 2025. In June a French court gave Carrefour six months to fix exactly these issues, under daily penalties.
>
> Worth 20 minutes to walk through your actual exposure? {{booking link}}
>
> {{You}}

### Email 2 — 3 days later, reply in-thread

> Hi {{FirstName}} — the one finding I'd fix first: {{specific violation}} on {{specific page}}. It blocks {{who it locks out, from the report}} — that's lost revenue before it's a compliance issue.
>
> Happy to show you the fix on a call, or just send it over in writing if that's easier. Which works?

### Email 3 — 4 days later

> {{FirstName}}, quick context on why this is on desks right now: EU member-state authorities began EAA market surveillance this year, fines run €5k–€1M depending on country, and disability associations can file complaints directly — that's how the Carrefour case started.
>
> If it's not you, who owns compliance/e-comm risk at {{Brand}}? Happy to send them the report.

### Email 4 — 5 days later, breakup

> Closing the loop — I'll leave the report with you. If accessibility comes up when you plan {{Q3/Q4}}, you have my details. One thing worth doing regardless: publish an accessibility statement; in France its absence alone carries a €25k/year penalty.
>
> Good luck with the season, {{FirstName}}.

Then stop. Re-scan and re-approach in 90 days ("re-ran the scan — still 14 critical").

## LinkedIn (no warm-up needed — start day 1)

**Connection request (no note converts better; if you use one):**
> Ran a WCAG scan on {{brand.com}} — found a few things your team would want to know about. Happy to share the report, no strings.

**After accept:**
> Thanks for connecting, {{FirstName}}. Short version: {{brand.com}} has {{X}} accessibility violations against the standard the EU now enforces for e-commerce (the EAA — applies to non-EU brands selling into the EU too). I put the findings in a short report — want me to send it here or to your email?

**Content (2–3 posts/week, this is how you "start the trend"):**
- Post anonymised scan results: "Scanned 20 DTC fashion brands selling into the EU this week. 19 had critical violations. The most common: …"
- Explain one violation in plain English + the fix.
- EAA enforcement news as it happens (set a Google Alert for "European Accessibility Act" and "accessibilité numérique sanction").
- Document the journey openly — "week 3 of building an EAA compliance agency" posts get founders in your DMs, and you own the category before copycats arrive.

## Objection replies (for emails — full handling in 04-sales.md)

- **"We use accessiBe/UserWay."** → "Overlays don't change the underlying code, so audits find the same violations — and 25%+ of accessibility lawsuits in recent years hit sites running overlays. Your scan attached shows what an authority would see today, widget included."
- **"We're US-based."** → "The EAA follows the customer, not the company. If EU consumers can buy from you, you're in scope — same pattern as GDPR."
- **"Not a priority."** → "Fair — most brands file it until the first complaint letter. The audit takes 3 weeks; the injunction deadline the French court set was 6 months. I'd at least know your number of critical violations. It's in the attachment."
