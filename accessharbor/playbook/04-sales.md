# 04 — Sales: pricing, the call, objections, proposal

## Pricing (charge in EUR; anchor high, discount never)

| Offer | Price | Your margin |
|---|---|---|
| Compliance Snapshot (automated scan + readout call) | **Free** (lead magnet) | — |
| Deep Snapshot (10–15 pages, key flows, written exec summary) | €750 | ~100% (you run the tool) |
| **Full EAA Audit** (manual, EN 301 549, statement, re-test) | **€4,500–€9,000** by site size | 40–50% after partner |
| Remediation support | €1,500–€3,000/mo | 30–40% after partner |
| Monitoring & quarterly attestation | €500–€900/mo | ~80% (mostly your tool) |

Rule of thumb quoting the audit: €4,500 base + €1,500 per additional template/flow beyond home–product–cart–checkout (accounts, search, subscription, apps). A marketplace or multi-locale site starts at €9,000.

**The model:** free scan gets the call → the call sells the audit → the audit report sells remediation → remediation converts to monitoring, forever. Monitoring is the business; the audit is the door.

## The 20-minute discovery call

1. **(2 min) Frame:** "I'll walk you through what we found, what the law actually requires of you, and what I'd do in your position. If it makes sense to go further I'll tell you; if you're exempt I'll tell you that too."
2. **(5 min) The report, their screen:** show 2–3 violations *live on their site*. Turn on VoiceOver (Cmd+F5) on their product page — an unlabelled "add to cart" button reading "button" out loud sells better than any deck.
3. **(5 min) Their scope, ask:** EU revenue share? Which countries? Team size (microenterprise exemption check)? In-house devs or agency? Any complaints/legal letters so far?
4. **(5 min) The path:** audit (3 weeks) → prioritised roadmap → their devs or ours fix → statement + monitoring. Quote the range on the call. "Based on your site, this is a €X,XXX audit."
5. **(3 min) Close:** "I can have the audit team started Monday and findings to you in three weeks. Shall I send the proposal today?" Send it within 2 hours while you're still in their head.

## Objection handling

- **"Too expensive."** → "One German fine is €100k per violation; your scan shows 14. The audit is €6k and makes you defensible in three weeks. It's also the cheapest CRO you'll buy — you're currently turning away every screen-reader user at checkout."
- **"Our devs can fix what the free scan found."** → "They should — but automated scans catch maybe a third of the requirements. The law is assessed against the full EN 301 549 standard, manually. Fixing the scan findings and stopping is how brands fail audits with a clean scan."
- **"We'll wait until we get a letter."** → "You can — but post-complaint you're remediating on a court's deadline, not yours, like Carrefour. Pre-complaint, a documented in-progress program is your best defence and takes the panic out of it."
- **"We already have an overlay widget."** → Same answer as in outreach, plus: "If the widget worked, this scan would be clean."
- **"Is this even real / never heard of the EAA."** → Send the European Commission's EAA page and one Carrefour-ruling article. Never argue the law's existence; let the sources do it.
- **"We're too small."** → Check honestly: <10 staff and <€2M turnover = exempt. Tell them, leave the door open, ask who they know. Honesty here generates referrals.

## Proposal (keep to 2 pages)

1. **Findings so far** — 3 worst violations from the snapshot, screenshots included
2. **Scope** — pages/flows audited, standard (EN 301 549 / WCAG 2.1 AA), deliverables: audit report, prioritised remediation roadmap, accessibility statement, one re-test
3. **Timeline** — kickoff within 5 business days; findings in 3 weeks
4. **Price** — one number, one optional add-on (monitoring). No menus.
5. **Next step** — "Reply 'approved' and we start Monday." Invoice 50% upfront, 50% on delivery.

Send as PDF. Follow up at day 2 and day 5, then weekly.
