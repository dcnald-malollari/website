# 05 — The white-label delivery partner (do this in week 1)

You sell; a specialist firm audits and remediates under your brand. Without a partner you can only sell the automated snapshot — so line this up before your first discovery call, not after.

## Who to look for

- Independent accessibility consultancies and freelance auditors, 1–15 people. Big enough to deliver, small enough to want your deal flow.
- Credentials that matter: **IAAP CPACC/WAS certification**, audits performed against **EN 301 549** (not just US/ADA work), experience testing with real assistive tech (JAWS/NVDA/VoiceOver).
- Where they are: the IAAP member directory, LinkedIn search "accessibility auditor" / "WCAG consultant" (Eastern Europe, Portugal, and India have deep talent at rates that leave you margin), people answering questions in the a11y Slack community and #accessibility on LinkedIn, W3C WAI mailing lists.

## The pitch to them (it's an easy yes)

You are not asking for a favour — auditors are chronically bad at sales and feast-or-famine. You're offering free deal flow.

> **Subject:** Deal flow for EN 301 549 audits — white-label
>
> Hi {{Name}}, I run AccessHarbor — we generate EAA compliance leads for e-commerce brands selling into the EU (cold outbound with automated scan reports; it converts well).
>
> I'm looking for one audit partner to deliver manual EN 301 549 audits and remediation support under our brand, or co-branded if you prefer. You audit; we handle prospecting, sales, PM, and the client relationship. Typical engagement: €4.5–9k audits, then monthly remediation/monitoring.
>
> Are you taking on white-label work? Happy to share the pipeline on a call.

Contact five; you need one to start (two eventually, for capacity and backup).

## Deal structure

- **Standard:** partner gets **50–60% of audit fees**, you keep 100% of snapshot/monitoring revenue (your tooling does that work). Alternatively agree their day rate (typically €300–600) and keep everything above it.
- **You** own: client relationship, brand on the report, invoicing the client. **They** own: technical accuracy, audit sign-off, their name inside the report if certification matters.
- Put in writing (one-page agreement): scope per engagement, turnaround SLA (15 business days for a standard audit), non-solicitation of your clients for 24 months, confidentiality, who carries professional liability for audit content (they do — confirm they hold professional indemnity insurance).

## Vetting (30-minute call)

1. "Walk me through your last EN 301 549 audit — what did the deliverable look like?" (Ask for a redacted sample. No sample, no deal.)
2. "Which assistive technologies do you test with, on which platforms?"
3. "How do you prioritise findings for a dev team?" (Good answer mentions severity × user impact × effort, not just WCAG numbering.)
4. "Turnaround for a 5-template e-commerce site?" (Expect 2–3 weeks.)
5. "Rate for white-label, and what's included in a re-test?"

Red flags: promises of "guaranteed compliance", overlay-widget resellers, no manual testing, refuses written scope.

## Until the partner is signed

You can still sell today: the free snapshot and the €750 Deep Snapshot are 100% yours to deliver with the scan tool. Book audits with a 3-week lead time — that's your buffer to close the partner.
