# 06 — Legal positioning: what you may and may not say

You are selling audit and remediation services, not legal advice. This distinction keeps you safe and — used right — makes you *more* credible, not less.

## Never say

- "You are breaking the law" / "You will be fined €X" (you can't know either)
- "Our audit makes you EAA-compliant" or "certified compliant" (no private company can certify EAA compliance; conformity is a legal presumption, not a stamp)
- "Guaranteed compliance" (the words of overlay-widget vendors; your partner will also refuse to sign it)
- Anything about a specific prospect's legal obligations stated as fact ("you must…") — frame as how the law generally applies

## Always say instead

- "Sites like yours **fall within the scope** of the EAA" / "the EAA **applies to services offered to EU consumers**"
- "Our scan found **X violations of WCAG 2.1 AA**, the standard referenced by EN 301 549" (a technical finding — always safe, always verifiable)
- "Meeting EN 301 549 gives a **presumption of conformity**" (the legally precise phrase)
- "For questions about your specific legal exposure, loop in your counsel — we're happy to brief them" (saying this *wins* deals; it signals you know where your lane is)

## Standing disclaimer

Already on the landing page and in every scan report; reuse it in proposals and the email footer:

> AccessHarbor provides accessibility auditing and remediation services. We are not a law firm and nothing in this document constitutes legal advice. Penalty figures reflect published national implementations of Directive (EU) 2019/882 and may change.

## Keep your claims database

Every factual claim you make in outreach must trace to a source. Current ones used in this kit — re-verify quarterly, laws move:

| Claim | Source to cite |
|---|---|
| EAA enforceable since June 28, 2025, all 27 member states | European Commission EAA page; Directive (EU) 2019/882 |
| Applies to non-EU companies serving EU consumers | Directive scope, Art. 2 |
| Carrefour ordered to fix site + app in 6 months (June 2026) | French court ruling — keep two news links in a bookmarks folder |
| Fines: DE €100k / FR €250k + €25k statement / ES €1M / IE €60k + prison | National implementing acts (Level Access and similar trackers maintain summaries) |
| Microenterprise exemption (<10 staff, <€2M) | Directive Art. 4(5) |
| 94% of top sites fail WCAG checks | WebAIM Million (annual — cite the year) |
| Automated tools catch only ~30–40% of issues | Widely published (Deque, GOV.UK studies) |

## Housekeeping

- **Your own compliance:** the landing page in this kit passes its own WCAG scan — keep it that way after edits (`node scan.js <your-url>`). An inaccessible accessibility agency is a screenshot waiting to happen.
- **GDPR basics for outreach:** B2B cold email to relevant business roles is generally defensible as legitimate interest in most member states, but include your identity, a real postal address once you have one, and honour opt-outs immediately and permanently. Keep your prospect data minimal (name, role, work email, domain).
- **Insurance:** once revenue is real (~month 3), get professional indemnity insurance and confirm your delivery partner carries their own covering audit content.
- **One hour with a lawyer** (~€200) before your first big close to review your proposal template and terms is money well spent.
