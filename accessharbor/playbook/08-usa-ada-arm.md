# 08 — AccessHarbor USA: the ADA arm

Same scan tool. Same reports. Same partner. **Different law, easier market.** In the US, web-accessibility failures aren't a future EU risk — they're an active lawsuit industry, and the buyers answer their phones in your timezone, in English.

## Why this arm is a gift

You already built the whole machine for the EU. The US market runs on the *identical* standard:
- The **ADA** (Americans with Disabilities Act) is enforced against websites via **WCAG 2.1 AA** — the exact benchmark your scan already reports against. Zero new tooling.
- **3,948 ADA website lawsuits were filed in 2025** (up ~24% over 2024); counting state courts, **5,000+**. **~70% targeted e-commerce.** Among the top 500 online retailers, **35.8% got sued at least once in 2025.**
- **46% of federal cases were repeat defendants** — brands sued *again* because they never actually fixed the site. These are your hottest buyers.
- Most cases start with a **demand letter** (30–60 days to respond). Typical numbers: demand letter ~$5,000; out-of-court settlement ~$30,000; court judgment ~$85,000. California's Unruh Act adds **$4,000 per violation** plus attorney fees.

The pitch writes itself: *"Fixing this costs less than one demand letter, and you're in the exact profile that's getting them."*

## Who to target (hottest first)

1. **Already sued once** — public trackers (UsableNet, ADA Title III blog, court dockets) list defendants. A brand that settled and didn't remediate is a near-certain repeat target and *knows* the pain is real. Warmest lead there is.
2. **US e-commerce, mid-size, visibly flawed** — same DTC profile you already scan. NY, FL, CA plaintiffs file the most, so US-registered brands selling nationwide are all in scope.
3. **Overlay-widget users** (accessiBe/UserWay/AudioEye) — overlays are named in a rising share of suits; the widget is a false sense of safety, and your scan proves the underlying code still fails.
4. **Brands you already scanned that are US-based** — Rhone, Rothy's, Vuori, BÉIS, Cotopaxi, True Classic. Their reports are already generated. Re-pitch on the ADA angle immediately, no new scan needed.

## The one big advantage: the phone works

US brands have US support lines — no international dialing, no carrier blocks, your business hours. The "courier confirming a document" call script in `04-sales.md` works as-is; just swap the EAA framing for the demand-letter framing below.

## Objection replies (US flavor)

- **"We have an overlay / accessiBe."** → "Overlays are increasingly named *in* these lawsuits, not a defense to them — plaintiffs' firms specifically target sites running them. Your attached scan is what their expert would see, widget and all."
- **"We already got sued / settled."** → "Then you already know the cost. The repeat-suit rate is 46% because most brands settle without remediating — the audit is how you don't pay twice."
- **"Isn't the ADA only physical stores?"** → "Federal courts have applied it to e-commerce sites for years; 2025 had ~4,000 website filings. It's the single most-litigated area of the ADA right now."
- **"We're too small to be a target."** → "Plaintiffs file volume, not prestige — small and mid-size DTC brands are the bulk of filings because they're least likely to have remediated. Size is why you're a target, not why you're safe."

## Positioning guardrail (same as always)

You are auditors and remediation specialists, **not a law firm** — nothing you send is legal advice. You report findings against a technical standard and, via a partner, fix them. Cite lawsuit *statistics and public rulings*, never predict that a specific brand "will get sued." Same disclaimer on every report, email signature, and proposal.
